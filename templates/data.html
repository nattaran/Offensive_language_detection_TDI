OILD ِِِDataset

The Offensive Language Identification Dataset (OLID) contains a collection of 14,200 annotated English tweets using an annotation model. 32.9% of tweets were labeled as offensive and the rest of them are labeled as non-offensive. OLID was the official dataset used in the OffensEval: Identifying and Categorizing Offensive Language in Social Media (SemEval 2019 - Task 6). Hate Speech and Offensive Language Dataset

This dataset contains a collection of 25K tweets and had manually coded by CrowsFlower(CF) workers. Workers were asked to label each tweet as one of three categories: hate speech (5.8% of the tweets), offensive but not hate speech (77.4% of the tweets), or neither offensive nor hate speech (16.8% of the tweets). The complete information about this dataset can be find in Automated Hate Speech Detection and the Problem of Offensive Language. The complete dataset is available in "hate speech and offensive language dataset".
